# Daily Operational Check-in

Purpose
- Provide a concise, actionable snapshot of the organization's operational health at the start of each day.
- Surface blockers, incidents, and readiness concerns early to enable proactive remediation.

Scope
- Applies to all critical operational domains including tooling, vendor systems, incident response, and facilities support.

Owner and Stakeholders
- Owner: Operations Manager
- Stakeholders: CFO, CTO, Product, Security, and Facilities where applicable

Inputs
- Status of critical systems (uptime, latency, error rates)
- Open incidents and MTTR
- Key operational metrics (ticket backlog, deployment cadence, vendor incidents)
- blockers and required decisions

Outputs
- Daily health snapshot report
- Updated runbook or ticket with blockers and next steps
- Any follow-up tasks created for the day

Procedure
- Step 1: Collect data from monitoring dashboards, incident trackers, and key vendor status pages.
- Step 2: Assess system health for each critical domain; categorize as healthy, degraded, or down.
- Step 3: Identify blockers requiring cross-functional input; log decision needs.
- Step 4: Draft a one-page Daily Health Report summarizing: health status, incidents, blockers, and actions for today.
- Step 5: Publish the report to the ticket/story and circulate to stakeholders via the agreed channel.
- Step 6: Update the ticket as actions are completed or escalated.

Cadence
- Daily at the start of the business day (adjust by timezone as needed).

Metrics
- Availability of critical systems, MTTR for incidents, number of open blockers, time-to-resolution for blockers.

Documentation and Records
- Store the daily report in the operational docs repository or the task ticket for auditability.
- Version history for significant changes to the check-in format.
