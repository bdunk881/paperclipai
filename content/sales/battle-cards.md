# AutoFlow Battle Cards

## AutoFlow vs Zapier

**When prospect mentions Zapier:**

### Their Strengths
- Brand recognition and large marketplace (7,000+ integrations)
- Proven track record for simple trigger-action automations
- Large community and template library

### Our Advantages
- **AI-native:** AutoFlow builds workflows from natural language; Zapier requires manual setup of each trigger, filter, and action
- **Self-healing:** AutoFlow adapts to edge cases automatically; Zapier workflows break when conditions change
- **Pricing:** AutoFlow starts free, paid from $19/mo. Zapier starts at $29.99/mo with strict task limits
- **Complex workflows:** Multi-step logic in AutoFlow is a single natural language description vs. chaining multiple Zapier zaps

### Key Objection Handling
- "Zapier has more integrations" — AutoFlow covers the most-used integrations and adds AI intelligence on top. Most teams use <20 integrations.
- "We already use Zapier" — AutoFlow handles the complex workflows Zapier can't, and costs less. Start by migrating your most painful automations.

## AutoFlow vs Make (Integromat)

**When prospect mentions Make:**

### Their Strengths
- Visual workflow builder with branching logic
- Competitive pricing for high-volume scenarios
- Good for technical users who want granular control

### Our Advantages
- **No visual builder needed:** Describe workflows in plain English instead of dragging nodes
- **AI decision-making:** AutoFlow handles conditional logic that Make requires manual branching for
- **Lower learning curve:** Non-technical users can build automations immediately
- **Self-healing:** Workflows adapt without manual error-handling setup

### Key Objection Handling
- "Make is cheaper" — Make's $10.59/mo plan has operation limits. AutoFlow's $19/mo Flow tier is simpler with no per-operation pricing surprises.
- "We like the visual builder" — AutoFlow's natural language approach is faster for most use cases. The AI shows you what it built so you have full visibility.

## AutoFlow vs n8n

**When prospect mentions n8n:**

### Their Strengths
- Open source / self-hostable
- Developer-friendly with code nodes
- No vendor lock-in

### Our Advantages
- **Zero infrastructure:** No servers to manage — AutoFlow is fully hosted
- **Non-technical users:** n8n requires technical skill; AutoFlow works for anyone
- **AI-native:** n8n is code-first; AutoFlow is intent-first
- **Support included:** AutoFlow includes support; n8n self-host is DIY

### Key Objection Handling
- "n8n is free to self-host" — True, but hosting/maintenance costs add up. AutoFlow Explore is free with no infrastructure to manage.
- "We want control" — AutoFlow gives full visibility into what AI builds. You approve before it runs.

## AutoFlow vs Power Automate

**When prospect mentions Power Automate:**

### Their Strengths
- Deep Microsoft 365 integration
- Enterprise compliance and governance
- Included in some M365 plans

### Our Advantages
- **Cross-platform:** AutoFlow works across all tools, not just Microsoft ecosystem
- **True AI-native:** Power Automate's AI features are bolt-on; AutoFlow was built AI-first
- **Simpler pricing:** No per-user, per-flow complexity. One plan price.
- **Faster setup:** Natural language vs. Power Automate's complex flow designer

### Key Objection Handling
- "We already pay for M365" — AutoFlow complements M365 by automating cross-platform workflows that Power Automate struggles with.
- "Enterprise compliance" — AutoFlow runs on Azure infrastructure with enterprise-grade security.
